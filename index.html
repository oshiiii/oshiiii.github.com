ABOUT

Base

  • The base of the equipment was made using square hollow MS members (L: 510mm, B: 25mm, T: 2mm)
  • Six cross members of the same material were welded between the longer base members
  • Two for providing support in the middle as the maximum weight of the equipment and motor lies in the middle (L: 105mm, B: 25mm, T: 2mm)
  • Two for supporting the drive motor on the right edge (L: 105mm, B: 25mm, T: 2mm)
  • Two on the left edge mirroring the right ones to maintain structural balance and integrity (L: 105mm, B: 25mm, T: 2mm)

Drive

  • The drive motor was connected to a gearset of 14 and 28 teeth respectively to move the base.
  • The driven gear was mounted on the axle shaft (L: 290mm, D: 7mm)
  • Two MS blocks (20x20x20 mm) were made for the bearing to be kept
  • The axle was made to mass through press fit bearings on the MS blocks on the either side
  • Two Derlin Wheels (D: 90mm, T: 15mm) on the outside edges of axle were bolted on the outside threaded part of the axle.
  • Similar procedure without gear attachment was done for the left side.

Lead Screw

  • A MS plate (L: 162mm, B: 127mm, T: 2mm) was used. Punching operation was carried out and the motor to drive the lead screw was mounted on the plate.
  • Two SS plates were punched to place the bearing for the lead screw:
  • One plate on top of the lead screw to press fit (L: 60mm, B: 31mm, T: 10mm)
  • The bottom plate is used for bottom bearing of lead screw and welding of the U-channel (L: 138mm, B: 30mm, T: 10mm)
  • The lead screw (L: 1170mm, D: 12 mm) was press fit on either sides and the U-channel was welded on the bottom plate and the top plate was welded between the U-channel.
  • A bush (L: 40mm, D: 22mm) was placed on the motor where the lead screw gets connected to the motor.
  • The bottom plate is connected to the motor mounted plate using two square hollow MS blocks (L: 130mm, B: 40 mm, T: 2mm)
